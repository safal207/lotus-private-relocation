# Partner Due-Diligence Follow-Up

Use this only after a reasonable response window or when a partner replies and asks what documents are required.

## Subject

Follow-up: International Introducer Partnership — Due-Diligence Documents

## Email body

Hello [Partner Team],

Thank you for reviewing my introducer partnership request.

Before I refer any client or share any personal data, I need to complete a short due-diligence and operating-boundary review. Could you please provide or confirm the following:

1. The exact legal entity that would sign the introducer or referral agreement.
2. Current trade-licence and DLD/RERA registration details for the executing brokerage.
3. The current introducer/referral agreement, including:
   - lead registration and duplicate-lead rules;
   - lead-protection period;
   - exact commission rate and calculation base;
   - payment trigger and evidence;
   - payout timing;
   - cancellation, refund, clawback, and failed-closing treatment;
   - repeat-purchase and related-party treatment.
4. The privacy and data-handling framework for international referrals, including:
   - controller/processor roles;
   - minimum client data required;
   - retention and deletion process;
   - cross-border transfer terms;
   - withdrawal and complaint route.
5. Confirmation that your licensed team owns property advice, advertising approvals, KYC, AML, sanctions screening, beneficial-owner checks, source-of-funds review, negotiation, documentation, deposits, and closing.
6. The name and role of the partnership manager who would own a small pilot.

I am not requesting confidential client or transaction information. A current agreement, public licence references, privacy documentation, and written confirmation of the operating process are sufficient for the initial review.

My goal is to begin with one synthetic handoff test, then move to a limited real-client pilot only after the process, consent, licence, data, and payment boundaries are clear.

Best regards,

Alex

## Internal send rule

Do not send this follow-up:

- within the first three business days after the initial message;
- after the partner has opted out;
- when a reply is already pending review;
- with any real client details attached;
- as evidence that a partner is approved.

Sending this message does not move a candidate beyond `DUE_DILIGENCE`.
