# Lotus Private Relocation — White-Label UAE Decision Desk

## Intended partner

Professional advisers and communities that already serve internationally mobile founders, investors, executives, and families.

## Problem

A client asks about moving to, establishing a base in, or buying property in the UAE. The primary adviser may understand the client's legal, tax, business, or family context but may not want to manage neighbourhood research, property-partner qualification, school dependencies, and multi-provider coordination.

## Proposed service

Lotus works behind the primary adviser to prepare a structured UAE decision brief and coordinate consented introductions to appropriately licensed or qualified providers.

## Pilot delivery

For each accepted case Lotus provides:

1. a structured intake using minimum necessary data;
2. a 60-minute discovery session;
3. a written decision brief;
4. risk, dependency, and evidence states;
5. a partner-category map;
6. partner-specific consent before any introduction;
7. handoff and acknowledgement tracking;
8. a named next-action owner.

### Reciprocal referral

- permitted only through a written agreement;
- compensation and conflicts disclosed where required;
- no payment for unlawful solicitation or unconsented personal-data transfer;
- regulated work remains with the responsible licensed professional.

## What the primary adviser retains

- ownership of its client relationship;
- legal, tax, immigration, accounting, or other regulated advice within its authority;
- approval of any co-branded or white-label client communication;
- direct handling of sensitive documents relevant to its work.

## What Lotus does not do

- present itself as a law firm, tax adviser, immigration consultancy, bank, investment adviser, or UAE brokerage;
- make visa, return, financing, tax, approval, or availability guarantees;
- hold deposits, purchase funds, or source-of-funds documents;
- transfer client information without explicit partner-specific consent;
- activate an unverified partner.

## Pilot acceptance criteria

A pilot is successful when:

- the adviser refers one synthetic and one consented real case;
- both parties agree the scope and data roles in writing;
- Lotus delivers the agreed brief on time;
- no client is confused about professional roles or compensation;
- the adviser confirms whether the service saves time and strengthens client retention.
