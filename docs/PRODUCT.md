# Product Definition

## Product name

**Lotus Private Relocation**

## Positioning

A private relocation and property-introduction desk for global founders, investors, and families who need a coordinated, evidence-led path from relocation intent to licensed local execution.

## Customer problem

International property and relocation decisions are fragmented across brokers, lawyers, tax advisers, banks, schools, immigration specialists, and local service providers. Clients often face:

- conflicting incentives;
- unclear responsibility boundaries;
- unverifiable claims;
- pressure to transact before due diligence;
- repeated sharing of sensitive information;
- weak visibility into status, next actions, and risk.

## Product promise

Lotus Private Relocation gives the client one structured coordination layer that:

1. clarifies goals, constraints, budget, timing, and family needs;
2. separates confirmed facts from assumptions;
3. introduces only relevant licensed specialists;
4. records consent and handoffs;
5. tracks decisions, evidence, risks, and next actions;
6. keeps transaction execution with the properly licensed party.

## Initial customer segments

- internationally mobile founders;
- owners of online and technology businesses;
- investors seeking a second home or operational base;
- families comparing property, schooling, residency, and lifestyle options;
- advisers who need a trusted property and relocation referral partner.

## Initial market corridor

**Source markets:** international founders and families, including people already based in Türkiye, Europe, the CIS, the UK, and the Middle East.

**Destination market:** Dubai and the wider UAE through verified licensed partners.

## Initial service packages

### 1. Private relocation discovery

A structured intake and needs map covering:

- relocation purpose;
- family composition;
- preferred timing;
- property use;
- budget range;
- schooling and lifestyle needs;
- business and travel requirements;
- adviser and compliance dependencies.

### 2. Licensed partner introduction

Consent-based introduction to the appropriate licensed or qualified provider, such as:

- real-estate broker;
- conveyancing or property lawyer;
- immigration specialist;
- tax adviser;
- bank or mortgage specialist;
- school or education consultant;
- relocation and move-management provider.

### 3. Decision coordination

A client-facing decision log with:

- confirmed facts;
- open questions;
- options under consideration;
- evidence sources;
- identified risks;
- owner and due date for each next action.

## Non-goals

The product is not intended to become:

- an unlicensed property marketplace;
- a repository of copied property listings;
- a substitute for legal, tax, immigration, banking, or investment advice;
- an anonymous lead-selling database;
- a channel for evading sanctions, taxes, reporting, KYC, AML, or source-of-funds checks.

## Success metrics

Early-stage metrics:

- qualified introductions accepted by both sides;
- partner response time;
- client time-to-clear-next-step;
- percentage of handoffs with explicit consent;
- percentage of material claims with supporting evidence;
- number of unresolved risks at each stage;
- partner-to-consultation conversion;
- consultation-to-transaction conversion, reported only by licensed partners;
- client satisfaction and referral rate.
