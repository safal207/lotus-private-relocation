# UAE Partner Due Diligence — 2026-07-21

## Scope

This review covers two candidate real-estate referral partners for the first Lotus Private Relocation corridor:

- D&B Properties;
- Gaia Living Real Estate.

The review is based on public information only. It does not replace a current licence search, corporate-document review, legal review, sanctions screening, contract review, or direct confirmation from the candidate partner.

## Decision

Both candidates remain in **`DUE_DILIGENCE`**.

Neither candidate is approved for:

- receiving real client personal data;
- representing Lotus as an active partner;
- publishing property inventory through Lotus;
- receiving deposits or transaction funds through Lotus;
- making legal, tax, immigration, banking, residency, return, or availability promises on behalf of Lotus.

The candidates may move to `PILOT` only after the authority, contract, data, compensation, and escalation gates below are completed.

- one section says a partner could receive **from 25%**;

- another states that commission terms depend on contribution and results.

Therefore, no percentage is treated as agreed, guaranteed, or forecastable until the signed contract defines the exact rate and calculation base.

## Activation gates

A candidate can move from `DUE_DILIGENCE` to `PILOT` only when all mandatory gates are evidenced:

### 1. Identity and authority

- exact legal entity confirmed;
- active trade licence confirmed;
- active DLD/RERA authority confirmed through an official source;
- permitted activities match the proposed client journey;
- authorised signatory confirmed.

### 2. Written referral agreement

The agreement must define:

- lead registration and acknowledgement;
- duplicate-lead process;
- lead-protection period;
- commission base and exact rate;
- payment trigger and evidence;
- payment deadline;
- refunds, cancellations, clawbacks, and failed transactions;
- repeat purchases and related parties;
- termination and post-termination treatment;
- governing law and dispute route.

### 3. Client and data protection

- minimum-data handoff agreed;
- client consent language agreed;
- controller/processor roles recorded;
- cross-border transfer basis reviewed;
- retention, deletion, access, and incident routes defined;
- no unsolicited transfer of contact details.

### 4. Compliance and transaction boundaries

- named owner for KYC, AML, sanctions, beneficial ownership, and source of funds;
- Lotus does not collect deposits or purchase funds;
- Lotus does not approve eligibility or compliance;
- property advertising remains with the authorised party and required permits;
- no visa, return, financing, availability, or approval guarantees.

### 5. Pilot controls

- one synthetic handoff completed first;
- one named partner manager assigned;
- acknowledgement SLA agreed;
- escalation path tested;
- status reporting demonstrated;
- no real client introduced until all blockers are closed.
