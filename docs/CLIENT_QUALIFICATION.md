# Client Qualification

## Purpose

Qualify whether a prospective client is suitable for a private relocation discovery call and, later, a consent-based introduction to a licensed or qualified partner.

Qualification is not legal, tax, immigration, banking, investment, compliance, or brokerage advice.

## First-stage intake

Collect only the minimum information required to decide whether a discovery call is relevant:

- preferred name;
- preferred contact method;
- current country or time zone;
- target destination;
- purpose of relocation or property search;
- broad budget range;
- expected timing;
- family, school, business, financing, or residency dependencies;
- language preference;
- source of the introduction;
- consent to be contacted.

Do not request passports, bank statements, tax returns, exact net worth, source-of-funds files, medical records, or children’s documents through the public intake form.

## Qualification dimensions

### 1. Intent

Examples:

- primary family relocation;
- second home;
- business base;
- long-term rental;
- property purchase;
- exploratory jurisdiction comparison.

### 2. Timing

- immediate: within 30 days;
- active: 1–3 months;
- planned: 3–12 months;
- exploratory: more than 12 months or unknown.

Timing indicates workflow priority, not pressure to transact.

### 3. Budget clarity

Record only a broad range at intake. Exact affordability, financing, tax treatment, and source-of-funds review belong to the relevant qualified provider.

### 4. Decision structure

Identify:

- primary decision-maker;
- other family or business stakeholders;
- advisers already involved;
- required approvals;
- unresolved disagreements or dependencies.

### 5. Service fit

A case may fit when the client needs coordinated introductions and decision tracking across property, relocation, schools, legal, tax, immigration, banking, or settling-in services.

A case may not fit when the person requests only copied property listings, guaranteed returns, guaranteed visas, anonymous transactions, regulatory avoidance, or urgent transfer of funds outside normal controls.

## Qualification states

### NEW

Enquiry received. No substantive qualification completed.

### CONTACTABLE

Valid contact route and consent to reply are present.

### DISCOVERY_READY

Purpose, destination, broad budget, timing, and major dependencies are sufficiently clear for a discovery call.

### NEEDS_CLARIFICATION

The case may be relevant, but one or more essential non-sensitive facts are missing.

### PARTNER_REVIEW_REQUIRED

A qualified partner must confirm whether the need falls within their permissions and competence before any introduction is presented as suitable.

### NOT_A_FIT

The request is outside scope, commercially unsuitable, or better served by another provider.

### COMPLIANCE_HOLD

The case must not progress because of suspected sanctions, fraud, coercion, hidden beneficial ownership, unexplained source of funds, document concerns, or attempts to bypass controls.

## Discovery-call output

A completed discovery call should produce:

- client goal statement;
- confirmed requirements;
- assumptions requiring verification;
- destination and location preferences;
- broad property requirements;
- family and business dependencies;
- professional-advice needs;
- risks and open questions;
- recommended partner categories;
- explicit next action;
- consent status for each proposed introduction.

## Scoring caution

A numerical lead score may help organise work, but it must not become an automated approval for regulated activity. High commercial value never overrides legal, consent, privacy, compliance, or suitability boundaries.
