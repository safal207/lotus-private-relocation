# Legal and Operational Boundaries

> This document defines product guardrails. It is not legal advice and must be reviewed by qualified counsel for every operating jurisdiction.

## Role

Lotus Private Relocation operates as a consent-based introduction and coordination layer.

Unless and until the relevant licence is obtained, the project must not present itself as:

- a real-estate brokerage;
- a property developer;
- a legal practice;
- a tax advisory firm;
- an immigration consultancy;
- a bank, lender, investment adviser, wealth manager, or fiduciary.

## Execution boundary

Only the appropriately licensed or qualified partner may:

- market or broker regulated property inventory;
- advise on legal title, contracts, ownership structures, taxes, visas, residency, mortgages, returns, or investments;
- collect deposits, reservation fees, purchase funds, or professional fees that belong to the executing provider;
- complete regulated KYC, AML, sanctions, source-of-funds, suitability, or compliance decisions;
- sign, reserve, submit, approve, reject, or transact on behalf of a client.

Lotus may coordinate communication and track progress, but coordination does not equal professional approval or transaction authority.

## Client claims

The project must not promise or imply:

- guaranteed residency or visa approval;
- guaranteed investment returns, rent, resale value, liquidity, or capital appreciation;
- guaranteed banking, mortgage, tax, or company-formation outcomes;
- guaranteed access to a property or unit;
- guaranteed transaction completion;
- special influence over a regulator, bank, developer, school, immigration authority, or government body.

## Introductions and consent

Before sharing a client’s identifying or contact information with a partner, record:

1. who will receive the data;
2. why the introduction is relevant;
3. what data will be transferred;
4. the client’s affirmative consent;
5. the date and channel of consent.

A referral agreement must not override the client’s privacy rights or create hidden exclusivity.

## Partner verification

Before activating a partner, record:

- legal entity name;
- licence or professional-registration details where applicable;
- jurisdiction and permitted activities;
- verified business contact details;
- conflict and compensation model;
- complaint and escalation path;
- data-processing role;
- agreement version and effective date;
- re-verification date.

## Compensation transparency

Where Lotus may receive a referral fee, commission share, marketing fee, or other benefit, the relationship must be disclosed when required and must never be described as independent advice if compensation creates a conflict.

Compensation must be governed by a written agreement. No fee is treated as earned until the contractually defined event is confirmed by the paying partner.

## Public communications

Public content must clearly distinguish:

- general information;
- verified factual claims;
- partner-supplied information;
- estimates;
- hypotheses;
- regulated advice provided by a named qualified professional.

Outdated programme thresholds, visa rules, tax rules, property availability, prices, and incentives must not remain published as current facts without a dated verification source.

## Prohibited use

The project must reject or escalate cases involving suspected:

- sanctions evasion;
- money laundering;
- concealment of beneficial ownership;
- tax evasion;
- forged or misleading documents;
- unexplained source of funds;
- bribery or influence claims;
- trafficking, exploitation, or coercion;
- attempts to bypass local property, immigration, banking, or reporting controls.

## Stop condition

When role, licence, consent, source, or authority is unclear, the workflow stops at **NEEDS REVIEW**. Uncertainty is never converted into permission by commercial pressure.
