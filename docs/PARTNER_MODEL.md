# Partner Model

## Objective

Build a trusted network of licensed and qualified providers that can execute the regulated or specialist parts of a relocation and property journey.

## Partner categories

- licensed real-estate agencies and brokers;
- property lawyers and conveyancing firms;
- immigration and residency specialists;
- tax advisers and accountants;
- banks, mortgage specialists, and payment providers;
- education and school-placement advisers;
- relocation, moving, insurance, and settling-in providers;
- family-office, wealth, and business advisers acting within their permissions.

## Minimum partner record

Every active partner must have a record containing:

- legal entity and trading name;
- country and operating jurisdiction;
- verified website and business contacts;
- licence or professional-registration evidence where applicable;
- named relationship owner;
- services and customer profiles served;
- languages supported;
- response-time expectation;
- compensation and disclosure terms;
- data-processing and confidentiality terms;
- escalation and complaint contacts;
- agreement start, expiry, and review dates.

## Qualification flow

```text
candidate identified
→ identity and licence checks
→ services and authority mapped
→ compensation and conflicts disclosed
→ privacy and consent flow agreed
→ sample case or references reviewed
→ written agreement signed
→ limited pilot
→ performance review
→ active / restricted / suspended
```

## Referral handoff

A valid handoff requires:

1. a qualified client need;
2. confirmation that the partner is relevant and permitted to help;
3. client consent to share the minimum necessary data;
4. a named owner on both sides;
5. a recorded handoff date;
6. acknowledgement from the receiving partner;
7. next-step and response-time expectation.

## Lead protection

The referral agreement should define:

- how a lead is registered;
- how duplicates are handled;
- the protection period;
- which legal entity and related parties are covered;
- what event creates commission eligibility;
- reporting obligations;
- payment timing and evidence;
- cancellation, refund, chargeback, and failed-closing treatment;
- repeat purchase and referral treatment;
- non-circumvention limits that remain fair to the client.

Lead protection must never prevent a client from choosing another adviser or withdrawing consent.

## Compensation models

Possible models include:

- a share of the executing partner’s earned commission;
- a fixed qualified-introduction fee where lawful;
- a fixed coordination fee paid by the client with clear scope;
- a white-label service fee paid by an adviser or agency;
- reciprocal referrals without guaranteed volume.

No percentage or amount should be published as universal. Each arrangement depends on jurisdiction, licence, service, written agreement, and the actual paying entity.

## Quality scorecard

Review partners on:

- licence and registration status;
- accuracy of claims;
- speed and clarity of response;
- quality of options presented;
- respect for client preferences and consent;
- absence of pressure or misleading urgency;
- compliance and documentation quality;
- issue resolution;
- client feedback;
- referral and payment transparency.

## Suspension triggers

Suspend or restrict a partner when there is:

- expired or unverifiable licensing;
- misleading claims or guaranteed outcomes;
- undisclosed fees or conflicts;
- pressure to transfer money before required checks;
- misuse of client data;
- repeated missed commitments;
- sanctions, fraud, bribery, or source-of-funds concerns;
- attempts to bypass Lotus consent or evidence controls.
