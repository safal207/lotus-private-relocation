# Data Handling Policy

> Product operating policy. Formal privacy notices, contracts, retention schedules, and jurisdiction-specific compliance require professional review before public launch.

## Principle

Collect the minimum data needed for a clearly defined relocation or introduction purpose, keep it only as long as necessary, and transfer it only with recorded consent or another valid legal basis.

## Public repository rule

This public repository must never contain:

- client names, email addresses, phone numbers, passports, IDs, addresses, or family records;
- bank, income, tax, wealth, source-of-funds, or transaction documents;
- private partner contracts or commission schedules;
- property reservation forms or signed agreements;
- private messages, call notes, or recordings;
- sanctions, compliance, or background-check results;
- access tokens, API keys, passwords, or internal system URLs.

Only synthetic examples and anonymised templates may be committed.

## Data classes

### Public

Approved website copy, methodology, public partner information, and non-sensitive templates.

### Internal operational

Partner status, service coverage, workflow state, task ownership, and non-sensitive performance data.

### Confidential

Client contact details, personal preferences, budgets, timelines, family needs, professional correspondence, and commercial terms.

### Restricted

Identity documents, financial records, source-of-funds materials, legal documents, sanctions or compliance information, health information, and children’s data.

Restricted information should normally be collected and stored by the licensed executing provider, not by Lotus.

## Intake minimisation

The first enquiry should request only:

- preferred contact method;
- current country or time zone;
- target destination;
- broad purpose of relocation or purchase;
- broad budget range;
- expected timing;
- whether family, school, business, financing, or residency support may be needed;
- consent to be contacted.

Do not request passport data, exact wealth, banking details, or source-of-funds documents in the public lead form.

## Introduction record

For each handoff, record:

- client identifier or internal case ID;
- receiving organisation and named contact;
- purpose of the introduction;
- minimum data fields shared;
- date and method of consent;
- who initiated the transfer;
- acknowledgement by the recipient;
- retention or deletion trigger.

## Access control

- access is role-based and least-privilege;
- sensitive exports are disabled by default;
- shared links expire where supported;
- personal devices and messaging channels are not treated as the system of record;
- every material access or transfer should be auditable;
- former team members and partners lose access promptly.

## Retention

Retention periods must be defined by data class, purpose, contract, and jurisdiction. When a case ends, retain only what is legally and operationally necessary. Delete duplicate documents, abandoned lead data, and expired exports.

## Client rights and corrections

Provide a clear route for clients to:

- request access to their data;
- correct inaccurate information;
- withdraw marketing or introduction consent;
- request deletion where applicable;
- understand who received their information;
- complain about data handling.

## Incident response

Suspected data exposure triggers:

1. stop further sharing;
2. preserve evidence without spreading the data;
3. identify affected systems and recipients;
4. revoke access and rotate credentials;
5. assess legal notification duties with qualified counsel;
6. notify affected parties when required;
7. document root cause and prevention actions.
