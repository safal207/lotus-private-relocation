# First Revenue Sprint — 14 Days

## Objective

Validate that clients or trusted advisers will pay for a structured UAE relocation decision brief before any property transaction closes.

## Primary target

- 3 paid Relocation Discovery engagements;
- 1 UAE Decision Map upgrade;
- 1 B2B white-label pilot conversation;
- 1 signed referral agreement with an activated UAE partner;
- zero real-client handoffs to a `DUE_DILIGENCE` partner.

## Offer used in the sprint

### Direct client

**Relocation Discovery — EUR 250**

- 60-minute structured call;
- written decision brief within two business days;
- one recommended next action;
- no regulated advice or guaranteed outcome.

### B2B adviser

**White-Label Discovery Brief — EUR 300 per accepted case**

- adviser retains the client relationship;
- Lotus prepares the brief and coordinates only consented introductions;
- synthetic rehearsal before the first real case.

## Daily execution

### Days 1–2 — proof package

- finalise offer, scope, and exclusions;
- prepare a synthetic Discovery Brief;
- prepare invoice and engagement wording outside the public repository;
- confirm payment method and tax/accounting treatment with a qualified adviser;
- create a private lead tracker.

### Days 3–5 — trusted-introducer outreach

Contact 10 organisations serving international founders or families:

- immigration and cross-border law firms;
- tax/accounting advisers;
- corporate-service providers;
- founder communities;
- relocation and education advisers.

Message goal: book a 20-minute fit call, not request client data.

### Days 6–8 — direct validation

Invite up to 10 relevant people from the founder, QA, AI, expat, and business network to a paid pilot.

Use a clear statement:

> I am testing a structured UAE relocation decision brief for founders and families who need clarity before speaking to brokers or immigration providers. The pilot includes a 60-minute session and a written action map for EUR 250. It does not include legal, tax, immigration, or brokerage advice.

Do not describe the work as free consulting or promise future commission-based discounts.

### Days 9–11 — delivery

For each paid client:

- collect minimum intake only;
- conduct the session;
- classify facts, assumptions, and professional dependencies;
- deliver the brief within two business days;
- request corrections before any introduction;
- obtain partner-specific consent separately.

### Days 12–14 — review

Record:

- contacts reached;
- replies;
- calls;
- paid engagements;
- delivery hours;
- objections;
- upgrade requests;
- partner requests;
- refunds or complaints;
- role-confusion incidents.

## Message tests

Test these value propositions separately:

1. **Avoid expensive wrong turns before relocating.**
2. **Turn a vague Dubai idea into a documented decision plan.**
3. **Give your adviser a structured property and family-infrastructure workstream.**

Do not change price and message simultaneously; otherwise the reason for conversion cannot be interpreted.

## Qualification gate

Accept a paid case only when the client:

- has a genuine UAE decision within roughly 12 months;
- accepts that Lotus is not the regulated executor;
- can provide a broad budget band and decision timeline;
- agrees not to send sensitive documents through public or unapproved channels;
- understands that the fee purchases a decision brief, not a visa, property, financing, or investment result.

## Stop conditions

Do not accept or pause a case when:

- the client asks for sanctions, tax, reporting, or source-of-funds evasion;
- the client demands guaranteed returns, approvals, visas, or banking;
- the intended partner is not activated;
- the required professional owner cannot be identified;
- the requested deliverable would be misleading without regulated advice.

## Decision after day 14

- **Continue:** at least two paid engagements and repeatable delivery.
- **Adjust:** strong call interest but no paid conversion; revise audience or outcome before lowering price.
- **Pause:** role confusion, unsafe requests, or delivery cannot be bounded.
