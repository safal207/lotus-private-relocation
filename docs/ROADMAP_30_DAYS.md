# 30-Day Launch Roadmap

## Goal

Validate one legal, repeatable referral corridor:

**International founders and families → UAE property and relocation → licensed local partners**

The first 30 days are for evidence, partner quality, workflow validation, and qualified conversations — not for building a large marketplace.

## Week 1 — Foundation

- publish product positioning and operating boundaries;
- define the client qualification form;
- define the partner verification record;
- identify 10 candidate UAE agency partners;
- identify 10 legal, tax, immigration, banking, school, or relocation specialists;
- request written referral and data-processing terms;
- create a synthetic demonstration case;
- define the private system of record outside the public repository.

### Exit criteria

- at least two partner candidates pass initial verification;
- at least one written referral agreement is under review;
- the intake form collects no unnecessary restricted data;
- every public claim has an owner and verification date.

## Week 2 — Offer and funnel

- publish a simple English landing page;
- create a Russian-language introduction page or message pack;
- define one primary call to action: private relocation discovery call;
- build partner-facing and client-facing message templates;
- create a case-status board;
- define consent, introduction, and follow-up steps;
- prepare FAQ and risk disclosures.

### Exit criteria

- one functioning lead route;
- one consent-recording mechanism;
- one partner handoff template;
- one client decision-log template;
- no public property listing is published without required partner authority.

## Week 3 — Controlled outreach

Target trusted introducers rather than mass-spamming affluent individuals:

- tax advisers;
- immigration lawyers;
- accountants and fractional CFOs;
- business brokers;
- founder communities;
- relocation advisers;
- international school and family-service networks.

Run a limited outreach batch and record:

- source;
- role;
- relevance;
- response;
- next action;
- consent status;
- partner ownership;
- outcome.

### Exit criteria

- 30 relevant adviser or community contacts;
- 5 meaningful replies;
- 3 partner conversations;
- 2 qualified client discovery calls or explicit referrals;
- zero unsolicited transfer of personal data.

## Week 4 — Pilot and review

- complete the first controlled client journey or synthetic rehearsal;
- measure handoff speed and failure points;
- review partner accuracy and behaviour;
- validate compensation evidence and reporting;
- document open legal and operational questions;
- decide whether to continue, restrict, or replace each partner;
- create the next 60-day roadmap from evidence.

### Exit criteria

- one complete end-to-end handoff rehearsal;
- all consent and evidence fields complete;
- material risks documented;
- no regulated action performed by Lotus;
- clear decision on the next market, offer, and partner set.

## Initial metrics

- verified partner candidates;
- signed or reviewed referral agreements;
- qualified adviser conversations;
- qualified client discovery calls;
- consent-complete handoffs;
- partner acknowledgement time;
- cases with unresolved legal or compliance questions;
- conversion between discovery, introduction, consultation, and partner-reported transaction stages.

## Do not optimise yet

Do not optimise for:

- traffic volume;
- scraped lead databases;
- a large property catalogue;
- automated recommendations without review;
- aggressive paid ads;
- unverified commission projections;
- multi-country expansion.

Optimise first for trust, evidence, consent, licensed execution, and a clean first journey.
